Pav Bhaji

Description

Pav Bhaji is a vibrant and flavorful street food dish hailing from Mumbai, India. This beloved comfort food features a spicy, buttery vegetable mash—known as bhaji—made with a medley of seasonal vegetables, tomatoes, and aromatic spices, all cooked to perfection. Served alongside soft, toasted pav (bread rolls), Pav Bhaji is not just a meal but an experience, often garnished with fresh onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lemon for added zest. Perfect for any time of day, it captures the essence of Indian street food with its rich flavors and inviting aroma.

Ingredients

  • Mixed vegetables
  • Onion
  • Tomatoes
  • Green chilies
  • Ginger-garlic paste
  • Butter
  • Pav Bhaji masala
  • Red chili powder
  • Turmeric powder
  • Salt
  • Coriander leaves
  • Lemon wedges
  • Pav bread
  • Butter
  • Chopped onions
  • Chopped coriander leaves

Steps

  1. Boil and mash the mixed vegetables.
  2. Heat butter and sauté onions until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; cook briefly.
  4. Stir in chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add spices and salt; mix well.
  6. Combine the mashed vegetables with the mixture and cook.
  7. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  8. Toast the Pav bread in butter.
  9. Serve hot with bhaji and garnishes.
